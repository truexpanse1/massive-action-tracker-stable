## Content Templates Explained

### Long-Form Facebook Ad

**Structure:**
1. Hook - Grab attention with a question
2. Problem Reframe - What they think vs. what it really is
3. Agitation - Describe the pain in detail
4. The Villain - What's really causing the problem
5. The Epiphany - Your solution
6. Proof & Credentials - Why you can be trusted
7. The Offer - What they get
8. Qualification - Who this is for
9. Scarcity - Why act now
10. Call to Action - What to do next
11. Warning - Cost of not acting

**Best For:** Facebook/Instagram ads, blog posts, sales pages

---

### Short-Form Facebook Ad

**Structure:**
1. Hook - Attention-grabbing opener
2. Problem - Quick pain description
3. Solution - What you offer
4. Proof - One credible result
5. Call to Action - Clear next step

**Best For:** Quick social ads, retargeting, awareness campaigns

---

### Email - Problem Agitation

**Awareness Stage Email**

**Goal:** Make them feel understood

**Structure:**
- Subject line (curiosity-driven)
- Personal opening
- Problem description (detailed)
- Agitation (what happens if it continues)
- Hope (there's a better way)
- Soft CTA (reply or click)

**Best For:** First email in a sequence, cold outreach, list warming

---

### Email - Solution Introduction

**Consideration Stage Email**

**Goal:** Introduce your solution

**Structure:**
- Subject line (promise solution)
- Reference their pain
- The epiphany (your discovery)
- The solution (how it works)
- Benefits (3-4 bullet points)
- Social proof (quick testimonial)
- CTA (learn more)

**Best For:** Middle of sequence, nurture campaigns, education

---

### Email - Godfather Offer

**Decision Stage Email**

**Goal:** Make an irresistible offer

**Structure:**
- Subject line (urgency)
- Recap problem & solution
- The offer (detailed)
- Bonuses (2-3 extras)
- Guarantee (risk reversal)
- Scarcity (genuine urgency)
- CTA (buy now)
- Warning (cost of waiting)

**Best For:** Sales emails, launch sequences, promotional campaigns

---

### Social Post - Story

## Troubleshooting

### "No dream clients found"

**Solution:** Create at least one dream client profile in the Dream Client Studio first.

### Reading level too high (Grade 7+)

**Solutions:**
- Click **Regenerate** to get a simpler version
- Add instruction: "Use even simpler words"
- Manually edit to replace complex words with simple ones

### Content doesn't match my offer

**Solution:** Use the **Specific Offer Details** field to provide exact details about your product, price, bonuses, and guarantee.

### Tone doesn't feel right

**Solution:** Try a different tone option. Each generates significantly different copy.

## The King Kong Framework

All content is based on proven frameworks from Sabri Suby and King Kong:

### The 17-Step Selling System

1. Call out your audience
2. Demand attention (big promise)
3. Back up your promise
4. Create intrigue
5. Shine light on the problem
6. Provide the solution
7. Show credentials
8. Detail benefits
9. Add social proof
10. Make your Godfather offer

### The Godfather Offer (8 Steps)

1. Lay groundwork (clear, valuable offer)
2. Add bonuses (desirable extras)
3. Stack the value (show total worth)
4. Reveal price (much lower than value)
5. Inject scarcity (genuine urgency)
6. Give guarantee (remove all risk)
7. Call to action (clear command)
8. Add warning (cost of not acting)
